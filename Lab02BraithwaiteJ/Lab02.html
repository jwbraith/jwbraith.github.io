THREE BOOKS

Over the confined summer of the year 2020 I read three short novels by famous science fiction & fantasy authors.

I first read Guards! Guards! by Terry Pratchett. My takeaway was of a world of dumb mobs held together by the efforts of vanishingly few principled individuals.

Next came Cat's Cradle by Kurt Vonnegut. This book did not replenish any belief in human beings I may once have had. It concerns the apocalyptic potential of human technology and our absurd apathy towards our own doom. Great read.

Ultimately I read Ubik by Phillip K. Dick. In case you are ever confronted by the question, the 'K' stands for 'Kindred'1. The story concerns anti-psychic agents who survive an assassination attempt and find reality begin to unravel around them. More than employing the narrative device of an unreliable narrator, the internal reality of the fiction is presented as unreliable. Quite disorienting.

These three books exhibit three shared characteristics

  • Fictional
  • Male Point of View
  • Envision Alternate Society

The single most important part of literary analysis is contextless ranking, which is as follows:

  1. Ubik
  2. Cat's Cradle
  3. Guards! Guards!
